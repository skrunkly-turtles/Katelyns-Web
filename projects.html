Hack the 6ix - veriFIRE

An edge-AI verification layer designed to work alongside a working smoke detector. It combines heterogeneous sensors as well as a live camera monitor equipped with a specially trained visual classifier to distinguish the environment into: [Fire, Combustable Smoke, Water Vapour, Normal]. Unlike regular smoke detectors, VeriFIRE takes in multiple contextual clues which allows it to give a smarter and more accurate reading on the state of the environment.
Running on a Raspberry Pi with QNX OS as its real time operating system, VeriFIRE is a plug-and-play model that can be easily detatched and used mobilely as well. Our specially trained, offline classifier was trained with real video footage, on a Yolo Nano v8, a CNN image-based recognition model.

UofT Hacks 13 - FlowState

An agentic environment of AI models which could communicate with each other freely and interconnectedly to pass information to one other. Users can input their behaviours and preferences, which will further influence the weightings of the agents.
During the session, FlowState continuously monitors the user's eyes, and screen, ensuring that the user is on-task and is on a page which is relevant to their work. Straying away from these two requirements for too long will cause Kevin to output a message of stern warning.
After the session, FlowState will analyze the data. It takes in duration, the user's previously inputted environment, the user's own self-reflection on how well the user focused, as well as any time the retina-detection or screenshotting software caught the user off-task. It then adjusts Kevin's behaviours to better suit the needs and quirks of the user.
